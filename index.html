Motivation

My need for a logical framework for life that also feels right has pushed me into a corner: I cannot accept a doctrine that misses one of those two points, and there is no such framework out there that also satisfies my criteria. The criteria are:

  • • Consistent with my experience and self-consistent
  • • Simple yet extensible to every facet of life
  • • Actively leads me to a happier life

A non-exhaustive list of alternatives can be found here, along with relational analyses to the proposed system.

This work is an attempt to bridge that gap for myself. It is my hope that sharing it with others will generate feedback to accelerate its development, thereby helping all those who choose to be involved.

By posting it on a public repo I'm imply the following:

  • • This is not the truth and it is not final - it is a work in progress
  • • This is meant to be shared, dissected, discussed and possibly scrapped in favor of a better option

General Summary

Everything is wavy and waves interacting with each other create additional dimensions. If waves resonate with each other they create structure. This pattern of simple structures building into more complicated ones is the fundamental driving force in our universe. Humans are the most complicated structures in the universe by this definition but we have the capacity to not only enhance our own structure with technology, we may eventually create stand-alone structures of greater complexity than ourselves. So the point of the universe, and particularly our place in it, is to create that next level of structure.

While that may be the purpose of the universe and humanity at large, it doesn’t have to be an individual’s purpose. There’s no denying that by and large, humanity is working very hard to achieve this goal and society generally rewards this behavior. Yet every individual is unique- a combination of predetermined potential and experiences that fill that capacity. Generally, an individual’s capacity for experience is left largely untapped, and we tend to narrow our range of experiences with age. We do not know our bodies, in terms of awareness as well as capacity for performance. Likewise our minds and ability to interact effectively with the external world are limited. They are limited because the dimensionality of an individual does not fit in nicely with society, and progress (subsequent resonance-driven structural density). Historically the variations of people that could contribute meaningfully to the continuation of structural development was limited, so many children died and a narrow gene pool persisted but we are experiencing a dimensional boom where the vanishing struggle for survival allows those who were traditionally unfit to flourish. With every passing year the amount of necessary activities that people must perform decreases, allowing us to create structural density at an exponentially increasing rate.

Happiness comes from jumping into this stream and creating persistent structures of your own. The more you make, the happier you’ll be! There are logical ways to go about doing this that are discussed. One key is that for structure to persist it must be repeated and supported, meaning it’s easier to do it in a group. Try to find something you can be a part of, and if nothing fits, figure out how you can create your own version. You might be surprised by who else it resonates with. Regardless, greatest happiness and maximal structural density occur together when all aspects of life are involved. We are omnivores, we can experience such a wondrous range of sights, sounds, smells and thoughts that by combining these things together is where we find our peaks. Sadly, as waves we can’t maintain peak intensities. Our search for happiness is a search for continual structural compactification that will allow us to reach a higher peak in the next cycle. There is no guarantee that any dimensional set that we are currently exploring will allow for significant compactification, ie will resonate with the external world, so we must maintain a basis that covers the entire range of our experiential abilities.

Summary of Framework

Wavy nature

Let's assume as a framework that our experience of the universe is wavy. Our current best descriptions of the universe are variants of String Theory. It is possible that String Theory is the limit of human ability to express its understanding of the world, as simply the limit of how we are able to experience it. Therefore the theory says as much about people as it does nature. Our sense of permanence is accomplished by solitons. Solitons, or repeating waves, form the basis of our experience.

Degress of Freedom and Phase Space

Our ability to experience and interact with the world can be decomposed into a finite set of dimensions. There are experiential dimensions for vision, such as up, down, dark, bright, or far and yellow. There are the dimensions for thought, which is a combination of repetitions of past experiences and impulses. There are the dimensions for our bodies and also external dimensions that we interact with. Let’s call the space of all possible dimensions phase space. At any time we are little waves repeating themselves in small sections of phase space, akin to inhabitants of Conway’s Game of Life.

Structure

Upon release of initial conditions, ie the big bang, there was no meaningful structure in the universe as it was by all indications perfectly symmetric. Over time, the symmetry collapsed and in its wake structures emerged. Let’s call structure repeating oscillations in phase space. Structures such as particles, light, then atoms, molecules, stars, and eventually people and the idea of god. The tendency of structure in the universe as it relates to evolution is always increasing complexity of structure or informational density. So what we see as evolution is the coalescence of simple structures into complex ones can be a measure of the acceleration of structural information density.

Symmetry Breaking and Fixed Points

Structure exists because waves tend to repeat themselves after they are created. Each time symmetry breaks, we can imagine a string being held down at a particular point. On a stringed musical instrument, holding the string at various points will create different notes. Each symmetry break creates structure and we will call it a fixed point.

The Bowl

The tendency of our entire observable universe to move in the direction of increased entropy lies in contrast with the series of extremely unlikely events that created ourselves. When we consider the phase space of our universe as a whole, if we put a randomly oscillating wave into that phase space (eg initial condition like the big bang), we have two choices when considering its evolution:

  • • The shape of the phase space is fixed, or predetermined
  • • The shape of the phase space evolves over time

Let's call the shape of the phase space The Bowl. The bowl is a convenient mental image because of the following point: Whereas the rest of the universe is undergoing fairly random chaos, our local region is becoming more and more coherent and orderly. The tendency of nature to fall into ruts of efficiency, such as the forming of stars, the forming of molecules, the evolution of those molecules into self-replicating structures. While every single event leading to our existence was random, in retrospect there is no denying that the universe is trending towards something. That something let’s call the bottom of the bowl. Just as unavoidably as a marble placed anywhere in it will end up rolling down the side of a bowl and will reach the bottom, so will a universe full of random chaos eventually explore the depths of its phase space bowl.

The Bottom of The Bowl

What is at the bottom of this phase space? If we look at the tendency of evolution through ourselves, the pattern that emerges is one of structures that lead to the creation of new degrees of freedom. Ape NFTs or metaverse gear has no meaningful implication in the “physical” world. As the informational density continues to increase degrees of freedom, humanity will have no choice but to interface more directly with technology to keep up. Is the bottom of the bowl just the start of a new one?

Applying the Framework to Ourselves

The First State - Singularity

There’s a lot of talk about enlightenment, the power of now, the loss of ego, etc. What they have in common is a very personal experience of the world and our place in it. Generally speaking, in these states there is a loss of differentiation between ‘me’ and ‘not me’. When we are born, we are automatically in this state, since the ‘me’ has not been created yet. Many people spend a lot of time trying to get back to this state and to hold it for as long as they can, believing it is the ‘solution’ to living life, or that some extra secrets lay there.

The First Basis - Duality

Our first and most basic contrast can be pleasure and pain, 'me' and 'not me', good and bad, love and hate. At some point after we are born, the symmetry of our experience collapses into one of these bifurcations. We are built to seek pleasure and avoid pain, yet clearly that is not the sole driver of humanity. However the symmetry breaks, a fixed point is created.
